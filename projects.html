Our Projects

Project Avani

Project Avani The main motive of the project is to create awareness among the youth about the environment, the impact of human activities on the environment and to work individually and collectively towards prevention and solution to the current problems. Through this essential subunit of NSS, we strive to make people aware about the fact that without protecting and taking care of our environment we’re putting too many lives in danger and we cannot take it for granted. To promote this idea the most recent Avani event, "Nature Walk" was organized, with the objective of helping people understand the various plant and animal species found in the area and also the measures that are currently being taken to extend the life of the wildlife as much as possible.

Project Indriya

Project Indriya’s vision is to remove discrimination against the differently abled and treat them with equity. The focus is on providing a better environment as well as a better future for them. Efforts are to be made to understand this community and identify their needs as well as problems. An event conducted under Project Indriya was named "Hear Signs". This was a reel making competition on the topic 'We sign for human rights', where participants were to deliver a powerful message using only sign language, no words. This really helped us establish the importance of the masses learning and using sign language so as to be inclusive towards the community which uses non verbal means of communication on a day-to-day basis.

Project Karakriya

Project kara-kriya is a recently launched project by NSS to promote a healthy lifestyle and overall well-being. The motive behind this project is to change the fact of sports being a luxury and it being confined to those who have access to it. The project strives to promote fitness, spread awareness about the benefits of healthy and a disciplined life. During the lockdown period, accommodating the high participation and competition in online games, a massive BGMI championship was conducted by this project. The event was a huge success and all the funds collected were donated to the Muskurahat Foundation.

Project Nirog

Project Nirog was thought of to initiate a revolution towards a healthy, disease-free and joyful society by providing a guide that is reliable as well as easy to comprehend and abide by. Nirog stands for health, happiness, compassion, kindness and togetherness. Not just physical health, we aim to promote the idea of overall well being by giving equal importance to physical, mental and emotional health. Nirog is a pillar that aims to assist the vulnerable groups of society who are having mental health issues, to destigmatize the mental health continuum, to promote help-seeking behaviors and debunk the myths pertaining to any health issues.

Project Sanrakshan

"Serving Animals, Serving Humanity." The aim of this project is to make Earth a better place for 'Our Friends in this Ecosystem': the voiceless, furry friends who are unable to stand for themselves against the inhumane behavior they face every day. Sanrakshan works towards raising awareness about putting a complete stop to animal cruelty, promoting animal loving practices such as adopting, looking after strays, and turning vegan. reducing the pain and sufferings endured by animals through our events and initiatives. We run a very innovative and informative series of short instagram videos on @nssmsi ‘s Instagram handle called “the Sanrakshan Diaries” to promote animal welfare, make sure to check those out!

Project Sanskriti

Sanskriti aims to make the ignorant aware about their own culture, so that they can respect the heritage of their country, and to collectively work to nurture these roots and strengthen them. The founding stone of this project was laid to raise awareness about the forgotten legacy and bring attention to various diverse cultures in India. To make people culturally aware, so that there is an appreciation for other’s values, customs, and beliefs and to meet them without judgment or prejudice. Project Sanskriti is trying to break down cultural barriers, build cultural bridges, and learn how to love, and appreciate those different from us. Project Sanskriti recently organized a quiz contest, “Loksatta Prashnotri”, held on the occasion of Republic Day. There were more than 100 participants, winners were provided with various gifts and a handmade portrait was given to the student, who secured first place.

Project Seva

Project Seva Conceptualized by one single NSS volunteer with a passion for the welfare of cows in India, Project Seva has now become one of the most active projects of our organization.The aim of this project is to encourage fellow feeling towards cows, to spread awareness about the situation of cows in India, to help feed stray cows and to provide them medical help. Many events were conducted for the same like fundraisers all the collected fund was donated in gaushalas supportive of our cause. A very engaging and educational webinar, “गौधन” was conducted by Seva, in collaboration with an organization with the same ambitions, spreading awareness about the condition of cows in our country and offering implementable solutions for the same.

Project Veerangana

Project Veerangana There’s something special about every woman in this world. If a woman has the strength to bear a child, she can also swing a sword when needed. Project Veerangana aims to empower women, help women acquire a sense of self worth, the freedom to make their own choices and the right to influence social changes for themselves and others. Project Veerangana has conducted multiple impressive events in the past on a large magnitude, one of which was Write-to-Unite, a writing competition which received more than a hundred entries and a rally organized in honor of International Girl Child Day 2021.

Project Vriddhi

“About the only thing that comes to us without effort is old age.” ~Gloria Pitzer.
Project Vriddhi aims to spread merriment and cheerfulness among the elderly community. Our objective is to build a community wherein people not only interact, communicate and take care of the old people but also learn life lessons and values from them. We aspire to develop the well being of the old people and work to empower them with basic digital literacy. Vriddhi arranges frequent visits to old age homes, and our volunteers conduct many lively and entertaining activities and events for the elderly there to bring a smile on their faces.
